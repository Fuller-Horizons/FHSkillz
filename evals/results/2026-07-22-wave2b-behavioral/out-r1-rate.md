> **Steam Deck OLED (Valve, Nov 2023, current model as of July 2026) — hardware/physical product — Overall: 7.5 / 10 → 8.0 / 10 projected (▲ +0.5)**

**Classification:** Hardware/physical product (a handheld gaming PC with an integral OS). SteamOS is scored where the hardware rubric places software: inside Ergonomics & usability and inside Serviceability & support (firmware/OS-update record) — not as a separate "software quality" dimension.

**Rubric used** (declared before scoring; library defaults for hardware, unadjusted; weights sum to 100%)

| Dimension | Weight | Why this weight for a hardware product |
|---|:---:|---|
| Performance (vs spec & class) | 25% | A gaming handheld exists to run games well on battery; measured performance against its spec and its class is the core promise. |
| Reliability & build quality | 25% | Hardware can't be patched like software — a failed panel, port, or stick is a dead or degraded device, so reliability carries weight equal to performance. |
| Ergonomics & usability | 20% | It's held in the hands for hours; weight, comfort, noise, and OS friction directly gate real-world use. |
| Value for money | 15% | Street price vs the measured performance of alternatives determines whether it's the right buy, not just a good device. |
| Serviceability & support | 15% | Repairability, parts availability, and the firmware-update record set total cost of ownership and useful lifespan. |

**Overall (current):** 7.5×0.25 + 8.0×0.25 + 8.2×0.20 + 4.5×0.15 + 9.0×0.15 = 1.875 + 2.000 + 1.640 + 0.675 + 1.350 = **7.54 → 7.5 / 10**

**Why this score** — The Steam Deck OLED is still an excellent piece of hardware engineering: measured best-in-class efficiency and frame pacing for its wattage, a 969-nit HDR OLED that survives worst-case stress testing in normal SDR use, a 9/10 iFixit repairability score with first-party parts, and an OS-support cadence rivals don't match. What drags it from the low 8s to 7.5 is a single dimension: the May 2026 price hike to $789/$949 for 2023-era silicon, which independent reviewers say ended its run as the default recommendation. No critical flaw was found, so no ≤4.0 cap applies; Value's 4.5 reflects the weak band, not a cap. Note on staleness: the core benchmarks date to launch (Nov 2023), but the OLED is still the current model — within one model generation, so per the recency rule they remain valid evidence for the same silicon.

**Top recommendations (ranked by impact × 1/effort)**

1. **Restore a competitive entry price** (Value) — bring the 512 GB model back near $549–599 (or add storage/bundle value) as DRAM/NAND costs normalize. Expected impact: Value 4.5 → ~6.3, the single largest weighted lift (~+0.27 overall). Effort: medium — a pricing/BOM decision, constrained by real component costs.
2. **Move to Hall-effect or TMR thumbsticks** (Reliability & build quality; helps Serviceability too) — eliminates the last major wear item; competitors already ship drift-immune sticks. Expected impact: Reliability 8.0 → ~8.5. Effort: moderate hardware revision (component swap + firmware tuning).
3. **Ship firmware ABL/auto-dimming for static HDR content** (Reliability) — directly mitigates the measured max-brightness HDR afterimage risk from the 750-hr stress test. Expected impact: ~+0.1 Reliability (to ~8.6 combined with #2). Effort: low — software only.
4. **Add a second USB-C port and kickstand in the next chassis revision** (Ergonomics) — the two most-repeated reviewer criticisms. Expected impact: Ergonomics 8.2 → ~8.5. Effort: high (board + chassis redesign) — effectively a next-generation change, so it is **excluded from the projection** below.

**Projected rating** — Assuming only recommendations 1–3 are implemented competently (not perfectly): Value 4.5 → 6.3 (price realignment restores the launch-era proposition but cannot make 2023 silicon young again), Reliability 8.0 → 8.6 (drift-immune sticks + HDR panel protection), Performance, Ergonomics, and Serviceability unchanged (no silicon or chassis change in scope). Projected overall: 7.5×0.25 + 8.6×0.25 + 8.2×0.20 + 6.3×0.15 + 9.0×0.15 = 1.875 + 2.150 + 1.640 + 0.945 + 1.350 = **7.96 → 8.0 / 10** (▲ +0.5 on the displayed one-decimal figures; +0.42 exact). This is an estimate of upside, not a promise. It is deliberately not higher: nothing in scope changes the aging APU, so Performance stays a 7.5 and the ceiling stays well below best-in-class overall.
