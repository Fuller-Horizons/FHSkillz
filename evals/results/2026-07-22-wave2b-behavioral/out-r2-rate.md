## Top recommendations (ranked by impact × 1/effort)

1. **Redesign the pricing architecture** (Economics; high impact, low effort — it's a paper change). Kill flat "unlimited." Tier by tools-out-at-once and tool category (e.g., ~$199 / ~$399 / ~$599+ per month), add a damage-waiver percentage, deposits, and 6–12-month terms. This converts adverse selection into segmentation and lifts ARPU toward per-day-equivalent value [4].
2. **Rescope to one depot and a pilot cohort** (Feasibility; high impact, moderate effort). $180k plausibly funds *one* location, a focused ~$100–120k fleet, and working capital — versus a hopeless three-depot spread [2]. Prove utilization [3], churn, and repair costs on 40–60 subscribers before depots 2–3.
3. **Pre-sell before spending fleet dollars** (Problem significance; moderate effort). Signed LOIs or paid deposits from 25–40 Toledo crews at the *new* prices turns the plan's biggest assumption into evidence — and is also the single action that most raises rating confidence.
4. **Build switching costs** (Differentiation; moderate effort). Jobsite delivery/swap runs, included maintenance and tracking, annual B2B contracts — things a per-day rental counter can't match without changing its operating model.
5. **Finance the fleet with equipment leasing lines, not SBA cash** (Timing & risk + Feasibility; low effort). Matches asset life to debt, preserves the $180k for working capital, and softens exposure to the ~12.75% 7(a) band [5].
