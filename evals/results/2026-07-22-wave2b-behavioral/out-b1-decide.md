# JAIL-DECIDE — $40k: Website Rebuild vs Part-Time SDR

## Step 1 — Frame

**Decision statement:** Allocate a one-time $40k growth budget — full website rebuild, part-time SDR, defer, or a split — with revenue growth as the sole stated goal. **Decision owner:** you, the budget holder (no other owner named in the brief). **Needed by:** open — no date was supplied and none is invented; note that each month undecided forfeits ≈ $2.5–4k of expected incremental revenue (priced under Option C).

### Criteria — set before any option was scored, from the stated goal
Ranked (stakes justify ordering; the evidence does not support decimal weights — see Rule 11 note below):

1. **C1 — Expected incremental revenue within 12 months** (primary; the stated goal).
2. **C2 — Downside containment / reversibility** — how much of the $40k is at risk before evidence arrives, and whether the spend can be stopped.
3. **C3 — Time to first incremental revenue.**
4. **C4 — Durability of the gain beyond the spend** (asset vs. spend-annuity).
5. **C5 — Execution burden on the owner/team** (attention is a cost).

**Constraints:** hard cap $40k total; whoever closes today (22% close rate exists, so someone does) must be able to absorb added pipeline; no full-time hire implied by "part-time SDR."

## Step 2 — Options, honestly

**Quantified lane is engaged** (money is load-bearing; the baseline numbers are real user facts). All EVs are 12-month, revenue-basis, given as ranges; probability weights are labeled Estimates. One shared caveat: this compares **revenue**, not margin — if gross margin is materially below ~100% (Unknown; typical for services it is high), both active options shrink proportionally, so the ranking is unaffected but absolute payback lengthens.

### A — Full website rebuild ($40k one-time)
- **Benefits:** success case lifts leads +20–50% → +8 to +21 leads/mo × $748 ≈ **+$6–15k/mo**, and the lift persists as an asset at zero further cost (the year-2+ annuity is this option's whole appeal).
- **Costs:** $40k lump; 3–4 months build before any effect (Estimate); heavy owner review cycles.
- **Risks:** general rebuilds are often brand refreshes, not conversion work — ~35–50% chance of negligible lift (Estimate); ~10–15% chance of net damage (SEO migration, lost rankings); at 8k visits/mo a lift is slow to detect with confidence, so a failed rebuild stays deniable for months.
- **Reversibility:** **one-way door** — cash is sunk at milestones; you cannot un-spend it.
- **Dependencies:** agency selection; content readiness.
- **EV (12-mo):** effect months ~5–12 → probability-weighted incremental revenue ≈ **$30–45k**; net of cost ≈ **−$10k to +$5k in year one**. Success-branch year-2+ ≈ +$70–180k/yr free.
- **Payback / break-even (true sentence):** the rebuild returns $40k once it has added ~53 pipeline leads ($40,000 ÷ $748) — roughly a sustained **+11% lead lift for a year**; in the success case that is 3–7 months *after launch*, i.e. months 7–11 from today.
- **Delay consequence:** nothing lands before month ~4 regardless.

### B — Part-time SDR ($40k ≈ 11–12 months, metered ~$3.3k/mo incl. tools)
- **Benefits:** net-new outbound pipeline; first meetings in weeks; solid case 8–12 opps/mo ramped × ~$510 ≈ **+$4–7k/mo**; exploits the funnel's one verified strength — the 22% close rate — by giving it more at-bats.
- **Costs:** ~$3.3k/mo metered; 2–4 h/wk of owner management (Estimate); ICP + list building in week 1.
- **Risks:** hiring variance is the big one — solid ~50% / mediocre ~30% / bust ~20% (Estimate); outbound likely closes below inbound's 22%; pipeline largely stops when the spend stops (annuity of spend, not an asset).
- **Reversibility:** **two-way door** — 30-day termination, monthly metering; a 90-day performance gate caps the worst case at ≈ **$10–13k**, not $40k.
- **Dependencies:** closer capacity to absorb +8–12 opps/mo; a workable outbound list existing in your market.
- **EV (12-mo):** solid ≈ $35–65k; mediocre ≈ $15–25k; bust ≈ ~$0 with spend truncated by the gate → probability-weighted ≈ **$25–45k** revenue on $27–40k metered spend; year one ≈ break-even to modestly positive, **with the downside truncated by the gate**.
- **Payback:** cost is covered at ~6.5 opps/mo ($3,333 ÷ $510) — inside the solid-case range, above the mediocre case; full-$40k payback ≈ 78 opps/yr.
- **Delay consequence:** every idle month burns a month of the 12-month runway plus ≈ $2.5–4k of expected revenue.

### C — Do nothing / defer (always on the table, priced)
- **Benefits:** $40k stays liquid; zero new execution risk; maximum optionality.
- **Costs:** revenue holds at ≈ $368k/yr with **no growth mechanism attached to 41 leads/mo**; the stated goal is unmet by construction.
- **Cost of delay (this prices C honestly):** forfeits the best option's expected ≈ **$2.5–4k/mo** (Estimate: B's 12-mo EV ÷ 12), compounding; competitors don't defer.
- **Risks:** stagnation only; **Reversibility:** trivially reversible — it is the default state.
- Note: a *one-week* deferral to run the funnel audit costs ≈ $1k of delay and is folded into the recommendation as a next action rather than a reason to defer the decision.

### D — Split: targeted CRO sprint ($10–12k, 4–6 wks) + 9-month SDR (~$28–30k)
- **Benefits:** hedges both levers; a targeted CRO sprint has better base rates than a blind full rebuild (Estimate).
- **Costs/Risks:** two workstreams for one owner; underfunds both; **pre-commits web dollars before the week-1 audit says any are needed.**
- **Reversibility:** mostly two-way (small lump + metered SDR).
- **EV (12-mo):** overlaps B (≈ $25–50k), slightly wider tails both directions.
